SECRETARIAT

Secretariat

United Nations Headquarters Building with Member State Flags

The United Nations Secretariat building at UN Headquarters in New York, with flags of Member States flying in front of the building.

The United Nations Secretariat serves as the chief administrative organ of the Organization. It provides studies, information, and facilities needed by United Nations bodies for their meetings. It also carries out tasks as directed by the Security Council, the General Assembly, the Economic and Social Council, and other organs. The Secretariat is headed by the Secretary-General, who provides overall administrative guidance.

At the United Nations Headquarters in New York, the Secretariat consists of departments and offices with staff members coming from some 170 countries. In the interest of securing the highest standards of efficiency, competence, and integrity, with due regard for the importance of recruiting the staff on as wide a geographical basis as possible, men and women are recruited from all over the world to serve the international community at the United Nations.
